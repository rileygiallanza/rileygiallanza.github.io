what are chromosomes, DNA, genes, and the human genome?
chromosomes
coiled chains of DNA that contain genetic information
DNA
a complex molecule that contains genetic information and makes up chromosomes
genes
segments of DNA capable of synthesizing proteins
the human genome
the complete instructions for making an organisms; consists of all genetic material in that organism's chromosomes

what have psychologists learned about temperament?
temperament, or emotional reactivity and intensity, is genetically influence; one form of a gene that regulates serotonin predisposes a fearful temperament and an emotionally reactive child
what is heritability and how does it relate to individuals and groups?
behavior genetics can estimate the heritability of a trait or the extent to which variation among individuals can be attributed to their differing genes; refers to the extent to which differences among people are due to genes
genetic influence explains about 66% of the observed variation among people's intelligence levels
as environments become more similar, hereditary becomes the primary source of difference
how is molecular genetics research changing our understanding of the effects of nature vs nurture?
"bottom-up" molecular genetics studies the molecular structure and function of genes
one goal of molecular behavior genetics is to find some of the main genes that together orchestrate complex traits such as body weight, sexual orientation, and impulsivity
epigenetics studies the molecular mechanisms by which environments can trigger or block genetic expressions
genes are self-regulating; our experiences lay down epigenetic marks, which are often organic methyl molecules that attach to a DNA strand and can prevent the DNA from producing proteins normally coded by that gene
epigenetics:
study of environmental factors that affect how our genes are expressed
molecular behavior genetics:
study of how the structure and functions of specific genes interact with our environment to influence behavior
behavior genetics:
study of the relative effects of our genes and our environment on behavior
