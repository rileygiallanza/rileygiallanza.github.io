how do arousal, expressive behavior, and cognition interact in emotion?
what are emotions?
a response of the whole organism, involving physiological arousal, expressive behaviors, and conscious experience
what is the James-Lange theory?
the theory that our experiences of emotion is not caused by the conscious perception of an event but rather the conscious perception of the physiological response to the event
what is the Cannon-Bard theory?
the theory that physiological and emotional experience occur simultaneously
to experience emotions, must we consciously interpret and label them?
what is two-factor theory?
the Schachter-singer theory that to experience emotion one must be physically aroused and cognitively label the arousal
what are negative emotions?
linked to the right hemisphere
what are positive emotions?
linked to the left hemisphere
does cognition always precede emotion?
we actually have many emotional reactions apart from our interpretation of a situation
what are the two pathways our emotional responses can follow?
high road?
a stimulus would travel to the cortex, then it would be analyzed and labeled before being sent out via the amygdala
low road?
a stimulus bypasses the cortex and goes straight to the amygdala; we process this outside our state of conscious awareness
how is emotional interpretation similar to that of getting a drug?
the psychological experience depends on whether you're aware or not; you can prime your sensory pathways for interpreting the signals from your body
what is the link between emotional arousal and the autonomic nervous system and how does arousal affect performance?
what is the yerkes-dodson law?
arousal affects performance in different ways depending on the task
what does the autonomic nervous system do in terms of this law?
it arouses the body when exposed to a stimulus and calms it down when it passes

people's tendency to be helpful when already in a good mood
what is positive psychology?
the psychological study of flourishing, with the goals of discovering and promoting strengths and virtues that help individuals and communities to thrive

self-perceived happiness of satisfaction with life; used along with measures of objective well-being to evaluate people's quality of life

ups and downs tend to balance out; we overestimate the duration of our emotions and underestimate our resiliency and capacity to adapt

what events provoke stress responses and how do we respond and adapt to stress?
what is stress?
the process by which we perceive and respond to certain events that we appraise as threatening or challenging; the opposite of happiness
how do catastrophes stress us?
usually known as toxic stress, damage to emotional and physical health can be significant after things like hurricanes, floods, or wildfires
how do significant life changes stress us?
stress among young adults is the highest
how do daily hassles stress us?
everyday annoyances add up and take a toll on health and well-being
what is general adaptation syndrome (GAS)?
Selye's concept of the body's adaptive response to stress in three phases— alarm, resistance, exhaustion
what happens during alarm?
the sympathetic nervous system activates your heart, your blood pressure, and muscles
what happens during resistance?
your adrenal glands pump hormones into the bloodstream in order to cope with the stressor
what happens during exhaustion?
you become more vulnerable to illness while your reserves deplete
what is the tend and befriend response?
under stress, people often provide support to others and bond with and seek support from others
the stress response system: when alerted to a negative, uncontrollable event, our A nervous system arouses us. heart rate and respiration B. blood is diverted from digestion to the skeletal C. the body releases sugar and fat. all this prepares the body for the D response
sympathetic; increase; muscles; flight-or-fight
how does stress make us more vulnerable to disease?
what is health psychology?
a subfield of psychology that provides psychology's contribution to behavioral medicine
what is psychoneuroimmunology?
the study of how psychological, neural, and endocrine processes together affect the immune system and resulting health
what general effect does stress have on our overall health?
stress tends to reduce our immune system's ability to function properly so that higher stress generally leads to greater incidence of physical illness
how does stress relate to HIV?
stress is correlated with a shorter progression from HIV
why are some of us more prone than others to coronary heart disease?
what is coronary heart disease?
the clogging of the vessels that nourish the heart muscle; the leading cause of death in many developed countries; Type As have twice the rate of heart attacks as Type Bs
what is type A personality?
friedman and rosenman's terms for competitive, headr-driving, impatient, verbally aggressive, and anger-prone people
what is type B personality?
relaxed and easy-going people
in what two ways to people try to alleviate stress?
what is coping?
alleviating stress using emotional, cognitive, or behavioral methods
what is problem-focused coping?
attempting to alleviate stress directly; changing the stressor or the way we interact with the stressor
what is emotion-focused coping?
attempting to alleviate stress by avoiding or ignoring a stressor and attending to emotional needs related to our stress reaction
how does a perceived lack of control affect health?
what is learned helplessness?
the hopelessness and passive resignation an animal or human learns when unable to avoid repeated aversive events
what is external locus of control?
the perception that chance or outside forces beyond our personal control determine our fate
what is internal locus of control?
the perception that we control our own fate
how can our self-control be depleted and why is it important to build this strength?
what is self-control?
the ability to control impulses and delay short-term gratification for greater long-term rewards
how effective is aerobic exercise as a way to manage stress and improve well-being?
what is aerobic exercise?
sustained exercise that increases heart and lung fitness
